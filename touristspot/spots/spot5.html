About Chaukhandi Stupa

The Chaukhandi Stupa is one of the earliest and most remarkable Buddhist monuments of Sarnath. Historical Background

The base of Chaukhandi Stupa dates back to the Gupta period (5th‚Äì6th century CE). Later, during the Mughal era, Govardhan, the son of Raja Todar Mal, added an octagonal tower to commemorate Emperor Humayun‚Äôs visit to the site in 1588 CE. Architecture & Design

  • Base Structure: Made of ancient bricks forming a solid stupa mound.
  • Octagonal Tower: Mughal addition built with red sandstone, showcasing Indo-Islamic influence.
  • Elevation: Offers a panoramic view of the Sarnath surroundings.
  • Condition: Well-preserved and maintained by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI).

Visiting Information

  • Entry Fee: No ticket required (Free Entry).
  • Timings: Open from sunrise to sunset, all days of the week.
  • Best Time to Visit: Early morning or evening for cool weather and golden lighting.
  • Facilities: Open grounds, benches, and peaceful walking areas nearby.

Location & How to Reach

Visitors often begin their Sarnath journey here before heading to Dhamek Stupa, Ashokan Pillar, and other monuments within the main complex.

Travel Tips
