About Ramnagar Fort

Ramnagar Fort is an 18th-century sandstone fort located on the eastern bank of the River Ganga, opposite Tulsi Ghat. Built around 1750 by Maharaja Balwant Singh, it was the royal residence of the rulers of Banaras (Kashi Naresh) and still houses the royal family.

The fort combines Mughal and Rajput architectural elements ‚Äî carved balconies, open courtyards, pavilions and river-facing galleries that offer dramatic views of the Ganga and the ghats.

Historical Background

  • Built: circa 1750 CE
  • Founder: Maharaja Balwant Singh (Banaras Kingdom)
  • The fort served as the administrative & ceremonial seat for the Kashi Naresh; parts still serve as private royal residence today.

Architecture & Design

The fort is constructed mainly from creamy Chunar sandstone with intricate carvings and Mughal-inspired arches. The design features broad courtyards, carved balconies and a series of rooms and halls that open to the river ‚Äî a harmonious mix of defensive formality and royal ornamentation.

Saraswati Bhawan Museum & Exhibits

The museum displays royal collections: vintage cars, palanquins, ivory work, ancient weapons, royal costumes, and handwritten manuscripts. A rare astrological clock ‚Äî over 150 years old ‚Äî is a special attraction.

Main Attractions Inside

  1. Saraswati Bhawan Museum ‚Äî manuscripts, vintage cars, royal memorabilia.
  2. Veda Vyasa Temple ‚Äî associated with the sage believed to have authored the Mahabharata.
  3. Darbar Hall ‚Äî royal audience hall used for ceremonies and Dussehra events.
  4. Dussehra & Ram Lila ‚Äî month-long traditional Ram Lila organised by the royal family attracting thousands.

Travel Tips & Best Time to Visit

How to Reach

The fort is about 14 km from central Varanasi areas. Common options:

  • Nearest railway: Varanasi Cantt or Varanasi Junction (reach by road/taxi).

Pooa Ghat (Added)

Pooa Ghat (local/lesser-known ghat area) ‚Äî often used by locals for rituals and sunrise/early-morning boat views. When visiting Ramnagar Fort, exploring nearby smaller ghats like Pooa Ghat gives you quieter, authentic experiences away from main tourist crowds.

Tips: Early mornings are best for photography and witnessing local rituals. Respect local customs and avoid loud behavior at ghats where rituals are in progress.

Fun Fact
