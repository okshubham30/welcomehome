Introduction

Chaukhandi Stupa is one of the most important ancient monuments of Sarnath, located about 8 km from Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh. It marks the spot where Lord Buddha met his first five disciples after attaining enlightenment at Bodh Gaya. The stupa serves as the symbolic beginning of the Buddhist Sangha (community of monks).

Historical Background

The original structure of Chaukhandi Stupa is believed to date back to the Gupta period (4th‚Äì6th century CE). It was initially built as a terraced temple to mark the place where Buddha reunited with his first disciples ‚Äì Kondanna, Bhaddiya, Vappa, Mahanama, and Assaji.

During the Mughal period, a square pavilion was added on top by Govardhan, son of Raja Todarmal, to commemorate the visit of the Mughal Emperor Humayun. This is why the stupa has both ancient Buddhist and medieval Mughal architectural influences.

Architecture

The structure of Chaukhandi Stupa is massive and made primarily of bricks and stone. It stands on a square platform with a cylindrical tower on top.

  • The base represents the original Gupta-era stupa.
  • The octagonal tower on the top was added during the Mughal period.
  • The monument combines Buddhist simplicity with Mughal ornamentation.
  • It is surrounded by manicured lawns and protected by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI).

Religious and Cultural Significance

For Buddhists, Chaukhandi Stupa is not just an archaeological site but a sacred symbol. It represents the moment of reunion between the Buddha and his disciples, marking the beginning of the spread of his teachings. The site is a place of meditation, peace, and reflection for pilgrims and visitors.

Restoration and Protection

The stupa is now under the protection of the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI), which maintains and preserves its structure. Periodic restoration and landscaping efforts have ensured that this ancient monument remains a major attraction in Sarnath.
