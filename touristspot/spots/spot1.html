About Kashi Vishwanath Temple

Kashi Vishwanath Temple, located in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, is one of the most revered temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. It stands on the western bank of the River Ganga and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva, making it one of the holiest pilgrimage sites in Hinduism.

The temple symbolizes the spiritual essence of India and attracts millions of devotees every year who seek liberation (moksha) and divine blessings from Lord Vishwanath.

History & Importance

  • The temple has been destroyed and rebuilt several times throughout history due to invasions.
  • The present structure was rebuilt in 1780 by Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore.
  • The temple dome is covered with nearly 800 kg of gold, donated by Maharaja Ranjit Singh in 1835.
  • In 2021, the Kashi Vishwanath Corridor was inaugurated, connecting the temple directly to the Ganga River.
