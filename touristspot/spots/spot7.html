Overview

Sarnath is home to several international Buddhist temples built by Thailand, Japan, Tibet, Myanmar, and other nations. Each temple has its own distinct architectural style, symbolizing the global reverence for the place where Lord Buddha delivered his first sermon.

These temples have separate entry gates, located a little away from the main Archaeological Complex. Unlike the main complex, entry here is free for all visitors, and you can peacefully explore each temple at your own pace.

Temples and Their Features

Thai Temple

The Thai temple, known as Wat Thai, is famous for its golden statue of Lord Buddha and peaceful gardens. The temple complex also includes a monastery where monks live and study Buddhist scriptures. Visitors are welcome to meditate or walk around the serene compound.

Tibetan Temple

The Tibetan temple reflects the colorful architecture of Tibetan monasteries. Inside, you'll find vibrant wall paintings, prayer wheels, and a large statue of the Maitreya Buddha. The atmosphere is deeply spiritual, with chanting often heard throughout the day.

Japanese Temple

The Japanese temple, also known as Nipponzan Myohoji Temple, is built in simple white stone. It includes a peaceful meditation hall and a replica of the Dhamek Stupa, representing the connection between Japan and India through Buddhism.

Other International Temples

Alongside these, you can find Burmese, Korean, and Chinese temples ‚Äî each representing unique forms of Buddhist art and design. These temples make Sarnath a true global center of Buddhist spirituality.

Visitor Information

  • Entry Fee: Free (for all international temples)
  • Timings: Usually open from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM
  • Photography: Allowed in most temples, but be respectful of monks and prayer areas
  • Best Time to Visit: Early morning or evening for peaceful atmosphere
